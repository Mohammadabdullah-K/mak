Co2 Laser Resurfacing

  1. Home
  2. Co2 Laser Resurfacing

CO2 laser resurfacing is a cosmetic procedure that uses a carbon dioxide (CO2) laser to remove the top layer of skin and stimulate the growth of new, healthy skin. It is a popular treatment for wrinkles, age spots, acne scars, and other skin imperfections.

CO2 laser resurfacing is a more invasive procedure than other types of laser skin resurfacing, such as erbium YAG laser resurfacing. This is because the CO2 laser vaporizes the top layer of skin, while the erbium YAG laser only removes the top layer of skin cells. As a result, CO2 laser resurfacing can produce more dramatic results, but it also has a longer recovery time.

How does CO2 laser resurfacing work?

The CO2 laser is a high-powered laser that emits a beam of light that is absorbed by water in the skin. When the laser beam hits the skin, it vaporizes the water, which causes the top layer of skin to be removed. The heat from the laser also stimulates the growth of new, healthy skin cells.

CO2 laser resurfacing can be done in one or two stages. In the one-stage procedure, the entire treatment is done in one session. In the two-stage procedure, the first session is used to remove the top layer of skin, and the second session is used to smooth out the skin and improve the results.

What are the benefits of CO2 laser resurfacing?

CO2 laser resurfacing can produce dramatic results, including:

  • Reduced wrinkles
  • Reduced age spots
  • Reduced acne scars
  • Smoother skin
  • More evenly toned skin
  • Younger-looking appearance

CO2 laser resurfacing can also be used to treat a variety of other skin conditions, such as:

  • Keratosis pilaris
  • Lichen planus
  • Actinic keratosis
  • Precancerous skin lesions

What are the risks of CO2 laser resurfacing?

CO2 laser resurfacing is a safe procedure, but there are some potential risks, including:

  • Infection
  • Bleeding
  • Scarring
  • Hyperpigmentation (darkening of the skin)
  • Hypopigmentation (lightening of the skin)
  • Prolonged redness
  • Sensitivity to sunlight
  • Need for multiple treatments

It is important to talk to a qualified medical professional about the risks and benefits of CO2 laser resurfacing before you decide to have the procedure.

How much does CO2 laser resurfacing cost?

The cost of CO2 laser resurfacing varies depending on the size of the area being treated, the number of treatments needed, and the experience of the practitioner. In general, CO2 laser resurfacing is more expensive than other types of laser skin resurfacing, such as erbium YAG laser resurfacing.

What is the recovery time for CO2 laser resurfacing?

The recovery time for CO2 laser resurfacing is longer than other types of laser skin resurfacing. You will need to wear a bandage on the treated area for several days, and you may experience some swelling, redness, and crusting. You will also need to avoid sunlight for several weeks after the procedure.

How long do the results of CO2 laser resurfacing last?

The results of CO2 laser resurfacing can last for several years. However, the results will gradually fade over time, and you may need to have touch-up treatments.

Is CO2 laser resurfacing right for me?

CO2 laser resurfacing may be a good option for you if you are looking for a dramatic improvement in the appearance of your skin. It is important to talk to a qualified medical professional about the risks and benefits of CO2 laser resurfacing before you decide to have the procedure.

Here are some additional things to consider before having CO2 laser resurfacing:

  • Your age. CO2 laser resurfacing is generally not recommended for people under the age of 18.
  • Your skin type. CO2 laser resurfacing may not be suitable for people with very dark skin.
  • Your medical history. If you have any medical conditions, such as a cold sore infection, you should talk to your doctor before having CO2 laser resurfacing.
